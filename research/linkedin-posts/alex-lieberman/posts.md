## Post 2 — Lessons from $75M exit

> 3 things that actually drove Morning Brew from 0 to $75M:
>
> 1. We made business news fun. (The product was genuinely good, not just "content.")
>
> 2. We referral-ized growth. Every subscriber could get free merch for referring friends. CAC dropped to ~$0.50.
>
> 3. We stayed obsessively focused. We said no to video, podcasts, events for 3 years. We just did email, really well.
>
> The things people think drove us: influencer marketing, viral moments, paid social.
> None of those were real drivers.
>
> The things that actually drove us: product quality, word of mouth, and saying no to distractions.

## Post 3 — On building a creator-led brand

> The shift I see happening in B2B:
>
> Old model: Company creates content → pushes it to audience
> New model: Creator builds audience → builds company around the audience
>
> The second model is 10x more defensible.
>
> If your company content lives on the company's handles, it's owned by the company.
> If your personal brand content lives on YOUR handles, it's owned by you — and follows you.
>
> For founders: the personal brand IS the moat.
>
> The audience you build on LinkedIn today is an asset that no algorithm change, no funding round, no competitor can take from you.

## Post 4 — On newsletter as B2B growth channel

> The most underrated B2B growth channel in 2024: a weekly email newsletter.
>
> Why?
>
> - You own the list (no algorithm risk)
> - Email open rates (20-45%) beat every social platform
> - Subscribers are self-selected — they raised their hand
> - It compounds: 1,000 subscribers today → 10,000 in 18 months with consistency
> - It creates a habit (people expect it every Tuesday at 8am)
>
> The newsletter is not a "content asset."
> It's a relationship asset.
>
> Every email you send is a deposit into a trust account.
> Eventually, you can make a withdrawal (sell, partner, hire, launch).
>
> If you don't have a newsletter, start one. This week.

**Format:** Text with bullet points  
**Theme:** Newsletter as B2B content and relationship channel  
**Approx. engagement:** 2,800+ likes  
**Why included:** Lieberman's newsletter expertise is uniquely credible; connects directly to B2B SaaS retention and trust

## Post 5 — On consistency in content creation

> The hardest part of building an audience isn't creativity.
>
> It's showing up when nobody is watching.
>
> Morning Brew sent emails every weekday for 18 months before we had meaningful revenue.
> I've posted on social media 300+ days in a row multiple times.
>
> People ask me: how do you come up with ideas every day?
>
> The answer: you don't wait for ideas. You build systems that produce ideas.
>
> My system:
> 1. Read for 30 minutes every morning (inputs drive outputs)
> 2. Keep a running note of things that surprised me this week
> 3. At 9am, pick the most surprising one and write about it
>
> Consistency isn't about discipline. It's about systems.

**Format:** Text  
**Theme:** Content creation systems, consistency  
**Approx. engagement:** 3,100+ likes  
**Why included:** Meta-framework for content creation that is directly replicable by any B2B marketing team
