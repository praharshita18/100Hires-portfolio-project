## Post 2 — LinkedIn posting frequency advice

> I've posted every single day on LinkedIn for 3 years.
>
> Here's what I've learned:
>
> 1. Consistency beats perfection. Post B+ content daily > wait for A+ monthly.
> 2. The first line is everything. If they don't click "see more," nothing else matters.
> 3. Your audience wants YOUR opinion, not a summary of someone else's.
> 4. The posts that feel too vulnerable/honest always perform best.
> 5. Short posts outperform long posts 80% of the time.
> 6. Lists get shares. Stories get comments. Questions get DMs.
> 7. Nobody cares about your company news. They care about your ideas.
>
> Stop trying to go viral. Start trying to be valuable.

**Format:** Numbered list  
**Theme:** LinkedIn content tactics  
**Approx. engagement:** 3,500+ likes  
**Why included:** Highly tactical, replicable principles — exactly the type of content useful for a playbook

## Post 4 — The "write like you talk" framework

> The best B2B content doesn't sound like B2B content.
>
> It sounds like a person talking to another person.
>
> Read your last blog post out loud.
> Does it sound like something you'd actually say?
>
> Or does it sound like this:
> "Leveraging best-in-class solutions to optimize stakeholder alignment and drive synergistic outcomes."
>
> Nobody talks like that.
> Nobody WANTS to read that.
>
> Write like you talk.
> Shorter sentences.
> Real words.
> Opinions.
>
> That's the whole content strategy.

**Format:** Text  
**Theme:** Content voice and tone  
**Approx. engagement:** 4,200+ likes  
**Why included:** High-resonance post; the "write like you talk" principle is core to DG's entire methodology
