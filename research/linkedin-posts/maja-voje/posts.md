## Post 5

We spent two years telling ChatGPT and Claude what *not* to do.
"Don't be generic." "Don't use jargon." "Act as a world-class expert and..."

Claude Opus 4.8 doesn't work that way.
It reads instructions literally and sizes its answer to exactly what you asked for.

So the old prompts - the ones we all copied in the ChatGPT era - now produce generic mush.

I rebuilt the 15 GTM prompts I run every week to match how 4.8 actually reads.

Four things changed everything:

→ Name the output. Not "review this," but "return a table: objection, severity 1-5, did I handle it."

→ Cap the length. 4.8 sizes to the task. Say "under 90 words" or it guesses.

→ Positive instructions only. "Don't be vague" does nothing. "Cut every easily, simply, powerful" works.

→ Action verbs. Return. Rank. Flag. Tear down. Each one tells 4.8 to ship something specific.

The 15, across 3 GTM stages - cold to hot:

❄️ COLD START: ICP / buyer hat · Market trend research · Call analysis · Positioning + differentiation · Strategic narrative

🔥 HEATING UP: Messaging + website teardown · Battle card · Permissionless value-prop outbound · Professional group post · Find relevant influencers + outreach

⚡ ON FIRE: Case study extraction · Content scaling machine · Sales deck opener · Loss analysis · Open a new market

They're plug-and-play. Copy, fill the brackets, run.

But they're useless without your real numbers in those brackets.

The prompt gets you 60%.
Your traction, your buyer's exact words, your dollar figures - that's the other 40%, and it's the part that makes the output yours.
