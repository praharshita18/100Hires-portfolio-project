## Post 1 — PLG vs. SLG: when to use each

> Product-led growth (PLG) is not the right strategy for every B2B SaaS company.
>
> PLG works when:
> ✓ The product delivers value quickly (first "aha" moment in <30 minutes)
> ✓ The end user and the buyer are the same person (or end user influences buying)
> ✓ Your ICP self-serves their own evaluation without needing a sales team
> ✓ The product is designed to spread virally within organizations
>
> PLG doesn't work when:
> ✗ You're selling to committees (complex enterprise sales)
> ✗ Your product requires onboarding and professional services to deliver value
> ✗ Your end user isn't the economic buyer and has no influence over purchasing
> ✗ Value is only realized after months of implementation
>
> Most companies try to add PLG as a layer on top of a sales-led model.
> That almost never works.
> PLG is a design principle, not a feature.

## Post 2 — SaaS pricing mistakes

> The 5 SaaS pricing mistakes I see most often:
>
> 1. Pricing based on cost, not value (your costs are irrelevant to the buyer)
>
> 2. Using seat-based pricing when usage-based pricing fits better (misaligns your incentives with customer success)
>
> 3. Charging per feature instead of per outcome (buyers want outcomes, not features)
>
> 4. Hiding pricing (people assume the worst when you hide it — and PLG customers won't self-qualify)
>
> 5. Never raising prices (your first pricing was a guess; you've learned since then)
>
> Most of these mistakes come from the same root cause: not talking to enough customers about pricing.
>
> Price is a conversation, not a decision you make alone.

**Theme:** SaaS pricing strategy  
**Approx. engagement:** 3,100+ likes

## Post 3 — The PLG content strategy

> PLG companies need a different content strategy than sales-led companies.
>
> Sales-led content goal: generate enough leads for sales to call
> PLG content goal: help users succeed so they upgrade, expand, and refer
>
> What this looks like in practice:
>
> - In-product content (tooltips, empty states, onboarding flows) matters more than blog posts
> - Documentation and help content is product content, not an afterthought
> - Case studies should be self-serve learning tools, not sales brochures
> - Community content is distribution: users teaching users
>
> The biggest PLG content mistake: creating awareness content without a self-serve path for people who are ready to try.
>
> Content that can't convert to trial is a brand investment, not a growth investment.
> Both are valid. Know which one you're making.

**Theme:** PLG content strategy  
**Approx. engagement:** 1,800+ likes  
**Why included:** Directly connects content strategy to PLG mechanics — uniquely valuable for SaaS content teams

## Post 4 — On freemium vs. free trial

> Freemium and free trial are not the same strategy.
>
> Free trial: "Try the full product for 14 days."
> Best for: complex products that need full-feature exploration to demonstrate value
>
> Freemium: "Use a limited version forever."
> Best for: products where viral spread or daily habit drives expansion
>
> The wrong question: "Should we do freemium or free trial?"
> The right question: "Where does value live in our product, and how fast can users experience it?"
>
> If a user can't experience real value in 30 minutes, free trial likely won't work.
> If users have no reason to come back after they finish a task, freemium won't work.
>
> Design the product for the motion. Then choose the pricing model that fits.

**Theme:** Freemium vs. free trial decision

## Post 5 — State of PLG (annual benchmark post)

> Every year I survey 200+ PLG companies for the State of PLG report.
>
> This year's most surprising finding:
>
> PLG companies that invest in community grow 2.7x faster than those that don't.
>
> Not surprising: community drives retention (you stay for the product, you stay longer for the community).
>
> Surprising: community also drives acquisition (users recruit users from their own networks).
>
> The unlock: community isn't a brand investment.
> It's a growth investment.
>
> The companies treating community as a PR activity are leaving their fastest growth channel on the table.
